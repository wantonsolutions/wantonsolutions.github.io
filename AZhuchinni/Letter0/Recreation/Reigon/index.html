California is composed of a variety of beautiful and hazardous biomes. San Diego and Los Angeles are part of a special and rare biome known as the chaparral. It’s a dry and dusty climate with occasional intense humidity blown in from the ocean which makes a thick fog on most fall mornings. Every hillside is covered in shrubs, cacti and non-native palm trees. I crashed my bike while enjoying a ride in the fog and among the trees, I thrown over the handlebars and skidded along the pavement in slick sandals until I hit an embankment and went tumbling into a shrub. This was my most intimate engagement with the chaparral to date (my bike remains unfixed [photo of bike]). San Diego is the most diverse biome in the united states, in addition to the Chaparral it is composed of the Pacific Ocean, San Diego river basin, and multiple desserts.

In one of my long FB posts I mentioned the Anzaberego dessert. It’s a harsh place but full of life, I went there to witness the superbloom, an intense botanical explosion in the desert. I wore my Neo Yokio Caprese Martini shirt on the trip, it did not protect my shins from the cacti. I recreated the scene from No Country for Old Men with Anton Sugar in the bathroom performing self surgery on the car ride home. It was worth it, if you find yourself out in the Californias you would be remiss to not visit the dessert.

Many of my exploits over the past year involve rock climbing, and the people I went with. Joshua Tree is a magical garden hidden out at altitude in the desert. Sand swept granite rises out of the desert, twisting and swooping into all kinds of strange and unique shapes. The contours and crakes on hard, gritty granite make for a climbers paradise. I went with three lab mates Erin McGinnin, Audrey Randall, and Rajdeep Das, each a character in their own right. Together we struggled up the notoriously difficult J-Tree boulders. I shredded myself on classic dubbed “Pig Fucker”, a flaking and powerful mantel onto an overhanging crack. For my success Audrey made me a figurine [picture of pig]. Yabo roof nearly dislocated my shoulder. We all got blown raw by granite in the wind, Raj dropped Erin on her ass with no pad, and Erin kicked me in the head on the car ride home.

The day before Joshua Tree I climbed in New Jack City, a dusty craig in the desert filled with snakes and beatniks. I climbed sketchy routes which tested my will on the slick granite. Erin nearly died disconnecting her rope from the belay loops while setting an anchor. Raj almost pulled me to serious injury by not knowing his gear, and Audry climbed the hardest route out of all of us and nearly degloved her finger by pulling on a bolt. The rock features of New Jack are jagged and slick granite. These features are typified by the areas premiere route Crooked Dick which was too busy to climb. Two climbing bums in their late 40’s Bill, and Ted cheered our team on while drinking beers at 9am and talking about the good old days. Bill picked up a 10 pound gopher snake and attempted to make every man woman and child touch the friendly beast before putting back to it’s home. Our crew was joined by two more UCSD students Daniel (a stern outdoorsman), and MJ ( a hilarious homosexual). Together the 6 of us did acro yoga and only sustained minor injuries. I managed to base Erin for a side star tick tock without killing her, an achievement for my usually taut hamstrings.

Mt. San Jacinto State Park is accessible via the united states highest rotating tram. The tram rises out of Palm Springs over near vertical precipes while spinning and blasting hits from the 50’s to the 90's including Staying Alive and I believe in a thing called love. Mt. San Jacinto (Tramway) is the cool, due to altitude, summer rock climbing roost for southern California. The park was filled with critters, chipmunks, squirrels, coyotes, and unseen bears. My team was Tammy (a heart surgeon), Erin (a colleague), and Ken (a lovable pervert). Ken, the most experienced climber, guided us through tramway park exposing us to some of it’s best climbing. A personable man, Ken chatted with every group we met, he suggested subtle and useful beta, bummed a beer, asked to swap shoes with people, and insisted for five minutes that it was a gentleman's duty to carry tampons in the wilderness to a group of seven women we bumped into. Ken also caught my head during a fall, and I still climb with this enigma of the man to this day. Tramway in the evening is a beautiful place, slowly descending into the sweltering desert from the mountain tops and watching the sun disappear over the crest of the valley is stunning. On the three hour drive home Tammy gave me the best directions I’ve ever been given.

Research

If there is anything that marks me thus far it's my work. Without a nod to the predominat expendeture of my time on earth a personal letter would be incomplete.

I needed to take classes again to satify my PhD requirements. My lab mates had a syncronized schedule so I hopped on and registerd with them. The first research project I embarked on was fun but a farce. For an embedded systems class I pitched the idea that farming could be automated if only we could solve the problem of harveting rype plants. For the sake of loose assosiation to pedagogy and satan I studied apples. Specifically if their rypeness could be detected automatically with the use of a camera and WiFi. The answer is a resounding no. 500 photographs of Royal Galla apples, a manilla tag studio, and an impressive image processing data set told me that apples, after picking, take months do develop signes of decay. Furthr they decompose at astoundingly different rates, and variations based on some known factors, like the farm of origin, and some still unkown to me.

Alex Snoeren is my advisor now. He makes Ivan look like a laxidosal slob in terms of academic precision. I prepare my language for 30 min before every meeting with him, and prepare for a barrage of questions at even the most slight out of place nuance on the charts I produce. It's a fantastic game and I'm a throughly better researcher than when my tenure at UBC expired.

I had the most fortunate of encounters. Maxwell Bland is a masters graduate of UCSD, he talks in riddles, paints his shoes, and acts like a child in the most abnoxcious of ways. He combines lazer like focus with a shallow understanding of computer science at large while simultanously providing insight at a level some assistant professors could only dream of. In some ways I acted as mentor to Max as his advisor was largly awol. We chatted and argued about philosopy, literature, music and memes. My favourte string to pull was mentioning DFW and watch him explode into a tangent about the fall of socieoty due to post-modern thought, and the stategies upon which we could save ourselves from nihilism. I found myself paired with max for a Networking group project in a class last3L00 Fall taught my advisor. I had suggestd that we look into programmable networking for a class project and considered using some of the hardware in the lab.

3pm Wednesday Max burst into my lab, sweat dripping down between his middle parted hair. "Stew, this is important I need you now". We walked out of the lab, down around the corner, to another proffessors office, and I was sat down without explaination. "This is Stew, another researching of programmable networking" Max decleared. I was about a quarter second from retreating back into myself in the face of real researchers in the feild, but Fortuna got the better of me and I decided to fully bullshit for a full hour abour research I knew very little about. Sitting across from me was a Cavium executive, and a professor whose name I still don't know. I pretened I understood programmable networking, and within an hour I was back in my lab. Within two weeks we had been gifted two bleeding edge programmble NIC's worth around 10K. I started a research project around the NIC"s, Max although enthusiastic about staring the project, never contributed a line of code to the repo.

It's been six months since that fateful day. I've proposed a novel operating system for isloating tennatns on light weight programmalbe networking devices. I have two members of the lab working with me on it and we will likely publishri to a major conference within a year. Max graduated with his masters degree and disapered to UICU. The life of a PhD student is mainly study, but the high enery healthy people that the masochistic job attracts leads to adventours activities. My rock climbing adventures are the most numerous, and too wide to recount all in a letter without becoming boring. However I will recount some of my adventures and the people I've done them with.

Multi-pitch climing is when you climb more than one pitch, a pitch is 30-45m, when you complete the first your belay partner follows up the leader and you continue climbing. This is how you climb sheer mountian faces thousands of feet high. Before comming to San Diego I had never multi pitch climbed before. My lab mate Audrey Randel, a red headed distance runner with a hair trigger personality and scary cutting marks on her forearm, suggested that we go climb Mt El Cajon after learning that I was a strong climber. Audrey pulled up to my house 5:00am that Sunday morning with red Subaru. On the way to the route I got chewed out for not waiting outside and asking what Reddit was. After a 30min drive and 45min uphill hike to the base of the craig I realized I could not find my belay device, a critical piece of climbing gear, which as it happens had fallen from my bag. We decided not to do the route as rational humans, tried a few single pitches, devised a way to pass our single belay devince back and fourth by swining it up to 40m between us on a rope and headed up. For the next 3 hours my life was at extreme risk as neither of us knew exactly what to do, and our single most critical piece of saftly gear was quite often haning from a literal thread. The feeling of fear climaxed when I nearly to a 80ft fall off an outcropping known as triton tower at 400ft. We ascended the mountian, and passing our belay device back and fourth lowered eachother down the mountain. I cried a little when my feet touched the ground. Audrey lectured me on why Prince Andrew and Pierre were both bastards and we ate some icecream afterwards. My sunburns took over two weeks to heal.

Rajdeep is a mad man. He has nearly killed me 5 times while driving to locations, especially an all day trip to LA where I nearly prayed for the first time in my life. In the same way ive seen him nearly fall to his death 3 times while climbing recklessly, and at the time of writing the man has a broken arm from flying headfirst over the handelbars of his mountian bike.
