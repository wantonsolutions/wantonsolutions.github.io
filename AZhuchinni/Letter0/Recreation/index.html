The life of a PhD student is mainly study, but the high enery healthy people that the masochistic job attracts leads to adventours activities. My lab mates with adventure in their bones incude Audry, Raj, Erin, MJ, and Anil. Allrisk members of this list I convinced to haul their asses up rock walls, and in turn got me to commit to fun and strange activities in turn. My rock climbing adventures are the most numerous, and too wide to recount all in a letter without becoming boring. However I will recount some of my adventures and the people I've done them with.

Multi-pitch climing is when you climb more than one pitch, a pitch is 30-45m, when you complete the first your belay partner follows up the leader and you continue climbing. This is how you climb sheer mountian faces thousands of feet high. Before comming to San Diego I had never multi pitch climbed before. My lab mate Audrey Randel, a red headed distance runner with a hair trigger personality and scary cutting marks on her forearm, suggested that we go climb Mt El Cajon after learning that I was a strong climber. Audrey pulled up to my house 5:00am that Sunday morning with red Subaru. On the way to the route I got chewed out for not waiting outside and asking what Reddit was. After a 30min drive and 45min uphill hike to the base of the craig I realized I could not find my belay device, a critical piece of climbing gear, which as it happens had fallen from my bag. We decided not to do the route as rational humans, tried a few single pitches, devised a way to pass our single belay devince back and fourth by swining it up to 40m between us on a rope and headed up. For the next 3 hours my life was at extreme risk as neither of us knew exactly what to do, and our single most critical piece of saftly gear was quite often haning from a literal thread. The feeling of fear climaxed when I nearly to a 80ft fall off an outcropping known as triton tower at 400ft. We ascended the mountian, and passing our belay device back and fourth lowered eachother down the mountain. I cried a little when my feet touched the ground. Audrey lectured me on why Prince Andrew and Pierre were both bastards and we ate some icecream afterwards. My sunburns took over two weeks to heal.

Rajdeep is a mad man. He has nearly killed me 5 times while driving to locations, especially an all day trip to LA where I nearly prayed for the first time in my life. In the same way ive seen him nearly fall to his death 3 times while climbing recklessly, and at the time of writing the man has a broken arm from flying headfirst over the handelbars of his mountian bike.

The single most pivitol life and family moment was when I presided over my sisters wedding. Before I migrated to San Diego i was invited to a pary by my older sister. I arrived on time to a basement apartemnt in east vancouver entirely filled with strangers. Three hours later when my sister arrived I was completely lit after having started the night doing boiler makers with burnouts. My sister asked me if I was going to give a speech at her weeding. I hopped up onto a chair and delivered a passonate treaste on the nature of marrage, coupling, and their entwinement to both the holy spirit and it's manifestation in Kelsey and AJ. I also went on to talk Steven Universe cosplay for an hour, little did I know I had been voulenterred to run my sisters wedding cerimony.

It was hinted at though conversation and texts that I would run the cerimony, but never formalized. In optimistic anticipation I planned a speach considering carefully my word choice with which to join the two families. Two weeks before my sister offically asked me, I bought a suit, penned a draft of the speech and flew to Vancouver. The hours prior to the wedding were a "Gong Show" the ceremony was to be held in AJ's back yard, and no one had planned on the setup past getting a few benches made. I hauled ass for two days setting up the back yard in preperation for the practice cerimony. The practice was in fact the real thing. I don't have the ability to merry people in Canada, so the dress rehersal formalized thier marrage in Law. I did not get to practice my part, the true officate walzted in with an air of pure athority, measured the room to check that no one was being forced into matrimony and presented the papers for signing. During the cerimony he read from a large leather bound book, which in my opinion was offical as fuck.

On the day of the wedding I co-opeed a large leather photo album from my parents closet and stuffed it with my notes for the cerimony. Fifteen minutes before I decided to make key revisions for another punch line right in the middle. Somehow in the chaos of the cerimony I also became the MC. Quite nervous I annouced that the cerimony would be starting soon, and lined up with the groomsmen at the front of the alter. As the music started playing I side stepped from the groomsmen and extracted my book from the closest makeshift pew. With perhaps the widest smile I've ever concocted I opened the book and took my position at the center of the alter. My inlaws and family had been totally unaware I would be preforming the cerimony so at this moment a huge look of confusion, and worry passed over the crowd. Kelsey and AJ approached, I welcomed them, and delivered.

To this date it's the second best presentation I've ever given, I got 3 laughs and 3 sets of tears from the audience. It was a real soul stirring piece to make the Grandmas happy and get the kids thinking. To my mind the only better talk I gave was my Masters defense.
