The first research project I embarked on was fun but a farce. For an embedded systems class I pitched the idea that farming could be automated if only we could solve the problem of harveting rype plants. For the sake of loose assosiation to pedagogy and satan I studied apples. Specifically if their rypeness could be detected automatically with the use of a camera and WiFi. The answer is a resounding no. 500 photographs of Royal Galla apples, a manilla tag studio, and an impressive image processing data set told me that apples, after picking, take months do develop signes of decay. Furthr they decompose at astoundingly different rates, and variations based on some known factors, like the farm of origin, and some still unkown to me.
