San Diego

San Diego is a young and interesting City, the purpose of this blog page is to collect information about San Diego and southern california so that I can visit interesting places. Moreover this should be a repository for links to books and to maps where interesting locations may be found.

Reading

Best of California's Missions Mansions and Museums

From the index this book can be judged by it's cover, its a collection of historic buildings in california. The latter part of the book discribes san Diego. The summary of San Diego touches on the basic history noted on the wikipedia page. An interesting point is that prior to mining becoming an indunstry in San Diego it barely had any development. The key mineral to be mined was borax out in the desert. This book contains references to Mission San Juan Capistrano, Mission de Alcala, the Estudillo House, Seeley Stable Museum. A large portion of the final chapter is dedicated to the museums of Balboa Park.

Mission San Juan Capistrano

Mission in San Juan just north of ocean side. It was set up by Juniperio Serra just like Mission Alcala. It was closed after the attack in San Diego where Luis Jame was killed in 1775. Adding to the history a magnitude 6 earthquate hit in 1812 and killed 40 people whene the roof fell on them.

Old Town - McCoy House

McCoy house is a historic home built in 1869, propery of James McCoy the sherif. The house was largely destroyed but reconstructed in 2000. The article stresses the importance of cattle especially their profitability in the form of Hide and Tallow

Balboa Park - Museum of Art

Some interesting points of history: Founded 1915. Opened 1926, Used as a Navy hospital during WWII and opened again in 1947. New wing 1975, 30M donation 1999 by local husband and wife.

Twenty Mule Team Museum

Borax museum in the town of Boron, this places gives an overview of the history of Borax mining in San Diego and how it injected a lot of money into the economy in the early 1900's.

Scotty's Castel - Death Valley Ranch

A strange catel in the destert, named after a man who never lived there but was a vicious con man. The castel was built by Alber Mussey Johnson who had real money after being swoond by the tall tails of Scotty mid swindle. This place could be a cool vist if I'm ever in the middle of no where.

Southern California's Best Ghost Towns

This seems to be a book by a very dedicated author who enjoys traveling around southern california and visiting ghost towns. Of particular interest are the maps in the book which are all hand drawn. The book itself is supped to be some kind reference manual for others who want to explore southern California by truck and visit many of the abanonded mining towns. Not all abandoned towns are for mining though, some are relocation camps during WWII and others are railroad towns. None the less all are abandond.

Mazanar

A gigantic Japaneese Internment camp out in the desert. Over 10,000 Japaneese were interend there for three years. This could be an interesting and sad visit into US war policy.
