Welcome to Cam Rub Fan Club The Game

Overview

Welcome to Cam Rub Fan Club the game. Everyone here is friends but they still want to win! To win the game collect tokens and get the most points. Token locations are generated by the "Next Token" generator on this page. This game is for two players, but feel free to play with more.

Characters

Cam Rub, Trent, Stew and Winnie, are the protagonists. They are paired into teams of two. One boy and one Girl each. Boys and girls think differently and in this game they move differently too.

In General boys are lighter and girls are darker colors (but it's really their physiques that separate them) Teams get color tokens with velcro on them. It does not matter what the teams are, except to have one boy and girl per team. (Winnie you can mouse over the characters to see yours).

Setting up the game

First setup the board. It consists of 4 laminated pieces each with a character on one corner. Arrange them as shown below so that the numbers increase clockwise around the edges. Each player also gets a score counter for tracking their tokens. Put one in front of each player. Determine how many tokens you want to play with. 11 tokens is standard, feel free to play for less or more.

Next choose a team, either orange or green. Then choose a boy and a girl for your roster. If you can't decide who plays which team color, or character decide by playing a mini game (ex: who can hold their breath the longest). Put both on the velcro of the team tokens. Place each character on their starting position. Next generate a token by pressing "r" or clicking the Next Token button. Place the token on it's color and number. The player to go first is decided with rock, paper, scisors, or another agreed upon game. Examples: Truth or Dare, Twister, Spin the bottle, ect.. (winnie more mouseover action :)

Playing a turn

Players alternate turns. On your turn you must move one character. Characters follow the movement rules detailed below (it's a bit like chess, and a bit like checkers). If a player lands on a token they collect it and put it on their score card for its value. Finally they generate a new token.

Scoring points

The color of the square a token is on determines how many points it's worth.

Blue: 1
Green: 2
Red: 3

Lower value colors are generated more often. This means the center of the board is most likely to generate a token. Be strategic about which tokens you collect.

Movement

Girls move diagonally, and Boys move vertically and horizontally. Characters move a single unit by default, but they can hopscotch off another character if they land on them. Hopscotching off a team member lets you make one additional move using the movement direction of the character you landed on. If a girl lands on a boy she can move one square vertically, or one horizontally and vice versa. If you land on an opponent you can choose to move either one or two square using the same rules as above. Below are examples of player movements.

Complex Movement

Just like checkers you can chain multiple jumps together. Feel free to bounce around until your hearts content and your strategy is exacted.

Figuring out the best way to chain together moves, and how to defend against them is the challenge of the game. The player who can strategize their movements the best will likely be the winner.

Winning the Game

When the final token has been collected the game is over. Each player sums up their tokens values to get their final score. The winner is the player with the highest score. This game is played for glory, but optionally the loser can be forced to do a humiliation.

Other Rules

Generating a token

When a token is generated it is placed on the board. If a token is generated onto player, that player gets the points right away and another token is generated

Return to home

To spice things up play the return to home variation. When you hopscotch off a player you can decide weather to take their move, or to send them home. When you send a player home they are moved directly to their starting position. If their starting position has a character on it the player that sent them home wins the game.
